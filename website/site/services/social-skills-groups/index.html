Social Skills Training Therapy Serving Upper Marlboro, MD

Our social skills groups are designed to provide a supportive and interactive environment where children can practice and enhance their social interactions. Each group is carefully structured to include activities that promote essential social skills such as sharing, cooperation, turn-taking, and effective communication. By engaging with peers in a guided setting, children have the opportunity to learn and reinforce their social skills while making connections with others who share similar experiences.

In addition to skill-building, our social skills groups foster a sense of belonging and community among participants. We create a safe and inclusive space where children feel comfortable expressing themselves and exploring their social abilities. Through role-playing, group games, and collaborative projects, children can experiment with new social strategies and receive feedback from peers and facilitators. This environment not only enhances their social skills but also builds self-esteem and confidence, enabling children to form meaningful relationships both within and outside the group.

What Families Can Expect From Social Skills Group Sessions

Every child develops social relationships differently, which is why Stabilizing Behaviors Consultations takes an individualized approach to social skills training therapy. Group sessions provide opportunities for children to practice social interactions with peers while receiving guidance from trained professionals who understand the unique challenges associated with autism.

Activities are designed to encourage participation in natural conversations, cooperative play, shared interests, problem-solving situations, and age-appropriate social experiences. Children learn through structured activities while also having opportunities to apply new skills during real interactions. This balance helps participants become more comfortable communicating with others in different settings.

Many families are interested in how social development is measured. Throughout participation in our social skills groups, observations help identify strengths and areas where additional support may be beneficial. Goals are adjusted as progress occurs so each child can continue building meaningful social connections at a pace that supports long-term success.

Supporting Social Growth Beyond Group Sessions

Social development does not stop when a session ends. One of the most important aspects of social skills training therapy is helping children use newly learned skills in everyday situations. Opportunities to practice may occur at school, during community events, while participating in recreational activities, or during family gatherings.

Caregivers play an important role in reinforcing social growth. Families may receive recommendations that encourage continued practice outside of sessions. Consistent reinforcement across environments can help children become more confident when initiating conversations, responding to peers, navigating group activities, and participating in community settings.

For children who may benefit from additional behavioral support, our ABA services can complement group-based learning by addressing individualized goals that support communication, behavior, and daily living skills.

Why Families Choose Stabilizing Behaviors Consultations

Families throughout Upper Marlboro, MD, choose Stabilizing Behaviors Consultations because of our commitment to evidence-based services and collaborative care. We recognize that meaningful progress requires more than a generic approach. Each child brings unique strengths, interests, experiences, communication styles, developmental needs, learning preferences, and personal goals.

Our team focuses on creating supportive opportunities where children can develop practical social abilities that may contribute to greater participation at home, in school environments, and within the community. Through structured guidance, ongoing assessment, family involvement, and individualized planning, we help children work toward social growth that is both meaningful and sustainable.

Frequently Asked Questions


What specific age groups participate in these peer sessions?

Our programs serve a wide variety of developmental stages, focusing primarily on school-aged children, adolescents, and teenagers who need structured behavioral support. How long does a typical group session run?

Most of our clinical group sessions run for sixty to ninety minutes weekly, keeping participants fully engaged without creating mental exhaustion. This timeframe allows our instructors to introduce a targeted skill concept and observe natural peer dynamics during cooperative games.


How do instructors track individual progress in a group environment?

Our clinical staff collects precise, data-driven metrics during every session to monitor specific behavioral objectives, tracking communication responses, peer interactions, cooperative tasks, and self-regulation markers. We share these progress summaries with families during regular parent training check-ins to maintain consistent strategy alignment across home and school environments.
